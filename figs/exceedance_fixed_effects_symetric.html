Interpretation. Exceedance risk (> 21 °C) was strongly governed by air temperature and streamflow. Each +100 CFS increase in discharge lowered the odds by ~18% (p < 0.001), while a +1 °C increase in maximum and minimum air temperature raised the odds by ~25% and ~29%, respectively (max p < 0.001; min p < 0.001). The pre-2017 vs post-2017 period effect was not significant (OR ≈ 1.16; p 0.21). Random effects were not significant, suggesting broadly consistent baseline risk and thermal sensitivity across sites. An AR(1) residual structure accounted for temporal clustering. Overall, the model explained ~61.1% of deviance (adj. R² ≈ 0.63).
